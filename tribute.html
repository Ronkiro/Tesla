About

"Nikola Tesla (July 10, 1856 to January 7, 1943) was an engineer known for designing the alternating-current (AC) electric system, which is still the predominant electrical system used across the world today. He also created the "Tesla coil", which is still used in radio technology. Born in what is now Croatia, Tesla came to the United States in 1884 and briefly worked with Thomas Edison before the two parted ways. He sold several patent rights, including those to his AC machinery, to George Westinghouse."

- Biography.com

Death


There are many mysteries surrounding Tesla's death

The recorded cause of Nikola Tesla's death was for Coronary Thrombosis. But there are still many mysteries and speculations about his death. His life is surrounded of mysteries!

"...However, Nikola Tesla’s death was shrouded in mystery and speculation was rife. It was rumoured that Tesla had not died from Coronary Thrombosis but was murdered by one of Hitler’s agents. Three days before Tesla’s death, he was believed to have been seen having a meeting with two of Adolf Hitler’s personal bodyguards including Otto Skorzeny, who is said to be Tesla’s alleged murderer. Otto Skorzeny was also one of Hitler’s spies who infiltrated the U.S Government’s Paperclip Project; a confidential military project for World War 2. Although Tesla’s autopsy file states Tesla died from Coronary Thrombosis, many of the original papers went missing after his death, before the U.S Government could get their hands on them. Tesla’s inventions would have been a significant threat to the Nazis, and this supports the theory he may have been murdered..."

- TOP5s
